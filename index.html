I build foundational and applied methods for AI measurement and the learning sciences.

I focus on the intersection of AI × Education × Psychometrics to achieve the simple, yet difficult, goal of education: helping students prepare for emerging opportunities in a world shaped by research, technology, and AI.

About

01 / 03

I'm a second-year M.S. student at the Stanford Graduate School of Education, working with Associate Professor Benjamin Domingue. From Fall 2026 I will be pursuing my Ph.D. with Professor Mutlu Cukurova at University College London.

I study the potential of large language models (LLMs) for student simulation combined with psychometrics, spanning applications in test design, teacher training, and personalized learning. I extend this work to measuring and developing teachers’ competency in AI, finding ways to help teachers navigate their experiences with rapidly advancing technologies.

Platform / Ecosystem

ResearchC

A research-industry ecosystem for university students.

I work on improving the existing research ecosystem in universities by building ResearchC, a Stanford-based platform that helps student researchers find collaborators, mentorship, and cross-departmental research opportunities.

Through ResearchC, I envision higher-education students leveraging research for their own goals — in academia or in industry — to maximise their learning opportunities.
